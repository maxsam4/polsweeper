Key Design Decisions

Master stored in Factory

The factory's masterOf mapping is the source of truth. The impl reads it via a cross-contract call. This means:

  • No storage in clones (cheaper deploy)
  • No initialize() call needed (no front-running risk)
  • Factory is the single registry of all accounts

CREATE2 Salt Design

salt = keccak256(master, index)

This makes addresses deterministic and predictable before deployment. Anyone can compute the address of any virtual account given the master and index.

Immutable Factory in Impl

The impl stores the factory address as an immutable. Since immutables are in bytecode (not storage), they work correctly through delegatecall.

No Access Control on Sweep

Anyone can call sweepPOL() or sweepERC20(token). Funds always go to the fixed master, so there's no attack vector. This lets the off-chain sweeper (or anyone) trigger sweeps permissionlessly.
